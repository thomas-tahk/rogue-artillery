# Research TODO

## Market Research: Roguelikes with Slay the Spire Formula

**Task:** Watch YouTube gameplay samples of roguelikes that follow the Slay the Spire formula to understand how other projects handle combat.

**Why this matters:**
Trying to figure out how the core Gunbound experience can fit inside a Slay the Spire roguelike structure without losing what made Gunbound great. The tension is that StS was clearly designed around *deterministic* outcomes — you know exactly what a card does. Artillery combat is the opposite: you can miss, terrain deforms in unpredictable ways, wind/weather can screw you over in unintuitive ways.

**The core question:** How do you reconcile non-deterministic artillery combat with the StS structure?

---

## Options Being Considered

1. **Live/real-time combat** — everyone moves and shoots constantly. Can be exciting but not the original vision.

2. **Discrete action set per turn** — e.g. Attack / Charge (strong attack, leaves you vulnerable) / Evade/Defend (limited uses per turn). This is the most StS-adjacent and worth exploring seriously.

3. **Something else** — this is what the research is for. Look for inspiration and case studies.

---

## Research Questions to Answer

- How do other StS-likes handle attacks that can miss or have variable outcomes?
- Do any StS-likes incorporate physics-based or positional mechanics?
- How do they keep player agency high while allowing variance?
- Is there a way to make non-determinism feel *intentional* rather than unfair?
